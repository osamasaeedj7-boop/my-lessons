The 5 Most Important Speaking Rules

Rule 1: Do not answer too fast

Use a natural opening phrase to gain one second and sound more natural.

Good phrases:

  • Well,…
  • Actually,…
  • To be honest,…
  • In my opinion,…

Example:

Q: Why did you choose this job?

A: Well, I chose this job because it has always been my dream.

Rule 2: Focus on one main idea

Do not mention too many things in one answer. Choose one idea and develop it.

Weak Answer

I play guitar and swim and walk and make reels and model and read stories.

Better Answer

In my free time, I mostly enjoy creating Instagram reels. I like planning ideas, choosing outfits, and filming content. It helps me express my creativity.

Rule 3: Build clear sentences

Try to make your sentence in this order:

Who Action Reason/Result

Examples:

  • I am a civil engineer.
  • I work for a company.
  • I enjoy this job because I help people.
  • This job makes me feel happy.

Rule 4: Do not repeat the same idea

If you already said the idea, add:

a reason
an example
a feeling
a result

Weak Answer

I like my job. I like this job. I am happy in this job.

Better Answer

I like my job because it combines creativity and technical work. For example, when a client is happy with my design, I feel satisfied.

Rule 5: If you do not understand, ask politely

In IELTS, do not stay silent.

Say:

  • Sorry, could you repeat the question, please?
  • Sorry, I didn't catch that.
  • Could you say that again, please?

Then answer the part you understand.

Q: How has social media influenced people?

Even if you do not understand everything, you can say:

I think social media affects people a lot today. For example, it changes fashion, lifestyle, and opinions.

Review: The 5 Rules

1

Do not answer too fast (use opening phrases).

2

Focus on one main idea per answer.

3

Build clear sentences (Who + Action + Reason).

4

Avoid repetition; expand with reasons or examples.

5

If you don't understand, ask politely.
